Recipes

These are a few of my favorite recipes that I make again and again in my own kitchen!

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

A classic cookie that's rich, chewy in the center, lightly crisp on the outside, and packed with chocolate.

What makes them special: The browned butter gives them a toffee-like flavor, and the sea salt on top makes every bite even better.

  1. Melt 3/4 cup butter until it smells nutty and turns amber. Let it cool.
  2. Whisk the butter with 1 cup brown sugar and 1/4 cup white sugar.
  3. Stir in 1 egg, 1 egg yolk, and vanilla.
  4. Fold in 1 3/4 cups flour, 3/4 teaspoon baking soda, and salt.
  5. Add chocolate chips and scoop onto a baking tray.
  6. Bake at 350°F for about 10 minutes. Finish with sea salt.

Vanilla Layer Cake

This cake is soft, fluffy, and perfect for birthdays or celebrations. The vanilla flavor is strong, sweet, and classic.

What makes it special: It uses both butter and oil for a tender texture, plus lots of vanilla for a rich flavor.

  1. Beat 1/2 cup butter, 1/2 cup oil, and 1 1/2 cups sugar until pale and fluffy.
  2. Add 4 eggs and 2 tablespoons vanilla.
  3. Alternate adding 3 cups cake flour mixed with 2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder and 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 1/3 cups buttermilk.
  4. Pour into two 8-inch pans.
  5. Bake at 350°F for about 30 to 35 minutes.

Chocolate Chip Banana Bread

This banana bread is warm, moist, and comforting. The chocolate chips on top make it extra sweet and fun.

What makes it special: Browned butter adds a deeper flavor, and ripe bananas make it soft and rich.

  1. Brown 1/2 cup butter in a pan, then pour it into a bowl and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Add 1/4 cup brown sugar, 1/4 cup sugar, 3 bananas, 1 beaten egg, 1 teaspoon vanilla, 1 teaspoon baking soda, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Mix in 1 1/2 cups flour.
  4. Fold in chocolate chips.
  5. Pour into nonstick dish and top with more chocolate chips.
  6. Bake at 350°F for about 50 minutes.
